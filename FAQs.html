<!-- 3) ARMOR MODELS -->

<h2 id="armor-models">Armor Models</h2>

<p>

EverQuest has famously stable (and occasionally complicated) armor graphics. Here‚Äôs some info about enabling various textures:

</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Velious Armors with Luclin Graphics</strong><br>

If you want to use Velious textures with Luclin models, it‚Äôs technically possible but unsupported. It can cause crashes or artifacting. Use at your own risk!

</p>

<p>

<code>LoadVeliousArmorsWithLuclin=FALSE</code>

</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Enabling Velious Armor for Classic Models</strong><br>

Add the following to the <em>[Defaults]</em> section of eqclient.ini in your THJ folder. Make sure <code>LoadVeliousArmor=TRUE</code> is there as well:

</p>

<pre>

LoadVeliousArmor=TRUE

LoadArmor17=TRUE  # Cloth

LoadArmor18=TRUE  # Ringmail

LoadArmor19=TRUE  # Scalemail

LoadArmor20=TRUE  # Leather

LoadArmor21=TRUE  # Chain

LoadArmor22=TRUE  # Plate

LoadArmor23=TRUE  # Monk

LoadArmor240=TRUE # Customized Helmets

</pre>

<p>

Each line above represents a specific armor type. Toggle individually if desired.

</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Obtaining Helmets with Custom Velious Graphics</strong><br>

Certain helmets (Thurgadin, Kael, Skyshrine) have special Velious graphics. For example:

</p>

<ul>

</ul>

<p>

Make sure your ‚Äúglamour stone‚Äù or ornament is the customized variant when buying or converting these!

</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>The TIM Layer (Toggle Improved Models)</strong><br>

<!-- 4) AUGMENTS, WHAT ARE THEY? -->

<h2 id="augments-what-are-they">Augments, what are they?</h2>

<p>

Augments are like WoW gems‚Äîlittle pebbles you slot into gear. Some require specific slot types. Inspect your item to see the available slots, pick up the aug from your inventory, then drop it in to socket it.

</p>

<p>

You can buy basic weapon augments from the Aug merchant in the Bazaar, and dropped armor augs from other players. You <strong>cannot</strong> slot two of the same gem type into one weapon.

</p>

<p>

Buy a Perfected Augmentation Distiller from the aug merchant to remove augs from gear. Note that <strong>Augments do not bind to you</strong>; you can trade them between alts!

</p>

<p><strong>Removing Augments:</strong><br>

Purchase a Perfected Augmentation Distiller. Left-click the augment in your item, and it will prompt removal while the distiller is in your inventory.

</p>

<h2 id="basic-macros---lootsell-macros">Basic Macros - Loot/Sell Macros</h2>

<p>

<h3>Loot Macro</h3>

Bind a key to ‚Äútarget nearest corpse,‚Äù and also use a macro:

</p>

<pre><code>/pause 5, /loot

/notify LootWnd LW_LootAllButton leftmouseup

/notify ConfirmationDialogBox CD_Yes_Button leftmouseup

</code></pre>

<p>

Now pressing that key will both target the nearest corpse and loot it automatically.

</p>

 

<p><strong>Sell Macro</strong></p>

<pre><code>/notify MerchantWnd MW_Sell_Button leftmouseup

/notify QuantityWnd QTYW_Accept_Button leftmouseup

</code></pre>

<p>Open the merchant window, click an item, press the macro to sell quickly.</p>

 

<p><strong>Destroy Macro</strong></p>

<pre><code>/notify IW_InvPage IW_Destroy leftmouseup

/notify ConfirmationDialogBox CD_YES_Button leftmouseup

</code></pre>

<p>

Open your character page, pick up an item, press the macro. If you have confirmations off, you can omit the second line.

</p>

<!-- 9) CAMERA VIEWS, FIELD OF VIEW, VIEWPORT -->

<h2 id="camera-views-field-of-view-viewport">Camera Views, Field of View, Viewport</h2>

<p>

During gameplay, you can view your character from different camera angles, zoom in/out, or pan up/down. Press <strong>F9</strong> to cycle views.

</p>

<ul>

 <li>First-person (default)</li>

 <li>Overhead (rotating)</li>

 <li>Rear (rotating)</li>

 <li>Overhead (fixed)</li>

 <li>Rear (fixed)</li>

</ul>

<p>

Press <strong>F10</strong> to toggle the interface, useful for screenshots.

</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Field of View</strong><br>

Use <code>/fov <number></code> (45 to 90) to adjust your field of view. For example, <code>/fov 45</code>.

</p>

<p><strong>Pan & Zoom</strong><br>

<code>Alt + arrow keys</code> or right-click drag to move the camera. <code>F12</code> toggles mouse panning.

</p>

<p>

Useful keys:

</p>

<ul>

 <li>Numpad 5/Home: Re-center</li>

 <li>Numpad 9/3 or PgUp/PgDn: Pitch up/down</li>

 <li>Numpad 7/1 or Insert/Delete: Zoom in/out</li>

</ul>

<hr>

<p><strong>Viewport Command</strong><br>

EverQuest was designed for 4:3. Widescreens can cause vertical cropping. You can run the game in 4:3 or use <code>/viewport</code> to adjust.

</p>

<p>

<b>Example Viewport Settings:</b><br>

<code>/viewport 171 0 1024 768</code> (768p)<br>

<code>/viewport 240 0 1440 1080</code> (1080p)<br>

<code>/viewport 320 0 1920 1440</code> (1440p)

</p>

From the Bazaar, use your <strong>Bazaar and Back</strong> AA, then type <code>/buyer</code> to open the Barter Buyer window. In the bottom-left search box, enter an item name and hit search to see if any version of that item is recognized.

</p>

<p>

For instance, if you have a base version of ‚ÄúShield of the Immaculate‚Äù but want to see the Legendary version, simply search for it in /buyer. You might see listings or be able to link it even if you don‚Äôt own it physically.

</p>

<p>

<b>Linking Items in Chat:</b><br>

Shift + Left Click an item in your inventory or on a merchant to create a clickable link in chat.  

This also works in the #myskills or #mystats windows if they support linking.

</p>
