<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Strength STR</button>
  <div class="content">
    <ul>
      <li><strong>  **3 STR** increases offense by **1**</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  First **74 points** of STR are not affected</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  Only affects melee (Archery and throwing use Dexterity with the same formula)</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  **1 STR** increases weight limit by **1**</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  **1 STR** increases attack by **0.9**</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  First **66 points** of STR are not affected</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  **1 STR** increases attack cap by **0.9**</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  First **66 points** of STR are not affected</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  **2 STR** increases blacksmithing skillup chance by **1%**</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  If you fail a combine, chance of skillup is halved</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  Higher INT or WIS takes precedence over STR with a **\-15% penalty** to skillup probability</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  Tradeskill gains use a **two-roll system**: Stats impact the first roll, while skill level impacts the second roll</strong></li>
      <li><strong>  **40 STR** increases endurance by **1** (Diminishing returns at **100** and again at **202** STR)</strong></li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="section-header">
    <h2>Advanced Stats</h2>
  </div>
  

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Spell Shield</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>Spell Shield is a worn item stat that reduces direct spell damage (non-DoT) before any spell runes or absorptions apply. It functions as flat percentage mitigation against incoming harmful spellcasts, including nukes, AE spells, and direct magical attacks.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>This effect stacks with other forms of mitigation such as Mitigate Spell Rune and Absorb Magic, but is applied first, helping to preserve rune-based defenses.</li>
      <li>Does not reduce DoT (damage-over-time) effects — DoT shielding handles that separately.</li>
      <li><strong>Example:</strong> With 25% Spell Shield, an incoming 1000 damage nuke is reduced to 750 damage before any runes are checked.</li>
      <li>Capped at 35% total mitigation.</li>
      <li>Useful against magic-heavy NPCs, spellcasting PvP opponents, or AE boss encounters.</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Important Notes:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Spell Shield does not absorb damage, it simply reduces the raw value of a spell hit.</li>
      <li>It's especially powerful when paired with threshold or mitigation runes that would otherwise deplete too quickly.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Shielding</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>Shielding reduces the minimum melee damage you can take from enemy attacks. It widens the damage range, making high-end hits statistically less likely over time.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>When an enemy makes a melee attack, the damage roll is between:</li>
      <li>Minimum Damage (min_damage) — the lowest possible value.</li>
      <li>Maximum Damage (max_damage) — the highest possible hit.</li>
      <li>Formula: $$$new_min_damage = min_damage * (1 - (Shielding % / 100))$$$</li>
      <li><strong>Example:</strong> With 25% Shielding, a mob normally hitting for 50–100 damage will instead hit for 37.5–100 damage.</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Important Notes:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Shielding only affects melee damage — not spells, dots, or abilities.</li>
      <li>This reduction is capped by ItemShieldingCap at 35%.</li>
      <li>Applies only to damage calculated with the min/max system.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Damage Shield</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>The Damage Shield stat causes attackers to take damage when landing a successful melee hit on the wearer. It requires an active damage shield from a spell or AA to function.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Item bonuses stack additively with spell/AA sources.</li>
      <li>Passively triggers when struck in melee combat.</li>
      <li>Does not affect outgoing player damage — only reflects damage onto attackers.</li>
      <li>Does not trigger on spell hits, procs, or ranged attacks.</li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Important Notes:</strong></p>
    <ul>
      <li>Attackers may mitigate DS damage through special defenses.</li>
      <li>Offhand attacks from dual-wielding NPCs or players may trigger reduced DS damage.</li>
      <li>Certain enemies might appear "resistant" to DS effects even when active.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">DoT Shielding</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>The DoT Shielding worn item stat reduces the damage taken from damage-over-time (DoT) effects. This includes effects such as poisons, diseases, curses, and any magical DoTs that deal their damage over a series of ticks.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Damage Shield Mitigation</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>The Damage Shield Mitigation worn item stat reduces the amount of damage a character takes when striking an enemy that has an active damage shield. These shields reflect damage back to the attacker when a melee hit lands, and this stat helps reduce that reflected damage.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>A flat damage reduction is applied first, subtracting a fixed amount from the incoming damage. For example, if the damage shield would normally reflect 60 damage and the attacker has 20 flat mitigation, the result would be 40 damage taken.</li>
      <li>A percentage-based reduction is applied next, reducing the remaining damage by a specified percent. Using the previous example, if the attacker also had 25% damage shield mitigation, they would then take only 30 damage (25% less than 40).</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="stat-card">
  <button class="collapsible">Avoidance</button>
  <div class="content">
    <p>Avoidance is a worn item stat that passively improves your chances of avoiding incoming melee attacks. It specifically reduces the attacker's chance to land a successful hit by making their attack roll less likely to succeed.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>The value of Avoidance is scaled by a server rule (PCAccuracyAvoidanceMod2Scale), which adjusts how impactful the stat is. By default, each point reduces the enemy's chance to hit by 0.1%.</li>
      <li>For example, 150 Avoidance would reduce an enemy's chance to hit you by 15% of their existing hit chance.</li>
      <li>If an attacker had a 30% chance to hit, Avoidance would reduce it to roughly 25.5% (30% × 85% = 25.5%).</li>
      <li>Avoidance only affects melee hits and does not reduce spell accuracy, procs, or ranged damage (like archery or throwing).</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
